# Core Resources & Reading List

## Primary Texts

### For Building Motives and Phrases
- **Jorge Variego**, *Composing with Constraints: 100 Practical Exercises in Music Composition* (Oxford, 2021) — The primary workbook for this curriculum
- **Arnold Schoenberg**, *Fundamentals of Musical Composition* (Faber, 1967) — Motive and phrase theory
- **Alan Belkin**, *A Practical Guide to Musical Composition* (free online)

### For Post-Tonal Cell-Based Composition
- **Miguel Roig-Francolí**, *Understanding Post-Tonal Music* 2nd ed. (Routledge, 2021) — Chapter 1 on cells
- **Joseph Straus**, *Introduction to Post-Tonal Theory* 4th ed. (Norton, 2016)
- **Vincent Persichetti**, *Twentieth Century Harmony* (Norton, 1961)

### For Contemporary Approaches
- **Olivier Messiaen**, *The Technique of My Musical Language* (Leduc, 1944)
- **Steve Reich**, *Writings on Music, 1965-2000* (OUP, 2002)
- **Béla Bartók**, *Mikrokosmos* (6 volumes) — Progressive cell-based études

### For Solo Wind Composition
- **Janet Halfyard**, ed., *Berio's Sequenzas: Essays on Performance, Composition, and Analysis* (Routledge)
- Analysis materials for Debussy's *Syrinx*, Varèse's *Density 21.5*, Hosokawa's *Vertical Song I*

### For Recorder Technique and Repertoire
- **Michael Vetter**, *Il flauto dolce ed acerbo* (Moeck, 1969) — Foundational avant-garde treatise
- **Walter van Hauwe**, *The Modern Recorder Player* 3 vols. (Schott, 1984-92)
- **Bennetts, Bousted & Bowman**, *The Quarter-Tone Recorder Manual* (1988)

## Online Resources

- **Recorder Map** (recordermap.com) — Basel Hochschule research project cataloguing techniques
- **CCBM** (Catalogue of Contemporary Blockflute Music) — 6,000+ contemporary works searchable by technique

## Reference Books (Already Owned)

- **Kostka, Payne, Almén**, *Tonal Harmony with an Introduction to Post-Tonal Music* (8th ed.)
- **Brent Auerbach**, *Musical Motives* — For analytical reference
- **Richard Cohn**, *Audacious Euphony* — Neo-Riemannian theory
- **Dmitri Tymoczko**, *A Geometry of Music*
