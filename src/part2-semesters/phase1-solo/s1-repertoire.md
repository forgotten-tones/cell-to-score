# Semester 1: Repertoire to Study

## Primary Works

### Debussy: *Syrinx* (1913)
- **Why**: The template for modern solo wind writing
- **Study focus**: Chromatic/pentatonic cell unification, tripartite form, breath as punctuation
- **Duration**: ~3 minutes
- **Score**: IMSLP

### Hosokawa: *Vertical Song I* (1995)
- **Why**: Eastern approach to breath-centered composition
- **Study focus**: Single-tone shaping, microtonal inflection, "calligraphy of sound"
- **Duration**: ~8 minutes
- **Score**: Schott

### Berio: *Gesti* (1966)
- **Why**: The landmark contemporary recorder work
- **Study focus**: Gesture as organizing principle, theatrical elements, extended techniques
- **Duration**: ~6 minutes
- **Score**: Universal Edition

## Secondary Works

### Varèse: *Density 21.5* (1936/46)
- Registral extremism, interval cycles, key clicks

### Telemann: *12 Fantasias for Flute* TWV 40:2-13
- Baroque form models, implied polyphony, fantasia structure

### Sciarrino: Flute works from *L'opera per flauto*
- Sound figures, timbral obsession, extreme dynamics

## Listening Without Scores

- Frans Brüggen recordings of Renaissance/Baroque recorder music
- Jeremias Schwarzer contemporary recorder recordings
- Recordings of Hosokawa's wind works

## Analysis Tasks

For each primary work, complete:
1. **Gesture identification** — List 3-5 recurring gestural shapes
2. **Formal mapping** — Diagram the structure
3. **Breath placement** — Mark where phrases breathe and why
4. **Motive extraction** — Identify the core motivic cells
